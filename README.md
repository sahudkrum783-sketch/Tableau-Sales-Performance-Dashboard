# 📊 Sales Performance Dashboard | Tableau Project

## 🔹 Project Overview
This Tableau dashboard project was created as part of my Data Analytics learning journey to analyze sales, profit, customer behavior, and regional business performance using an interactive visual storytelling approach.

The dashboard transforms raw transactional sales data into meaningful business insights that help identify:

- Profitable and loss-making regions
- Customer segments contributing maximum profit
- Monthly sales and order quantity trends
- High sales but low profit customers
- Region-wise sales contribution

This project helped me strengthen my skills in dashboard design, chart selection, and business insight generation using Tableau.

---

## 🔹 Objective of the Dashboard
The main objective of this dashboard is to evaluate overall business sales performance and profitability by answering the following questions:

1. Which region contributes the highest sales?
2. Which customer segment is most profitable across regions?
3. How do monthly order quantities relate to monthly sales?
4. Which customers generate high sales but low profits?
5. What is the percentage contribution of each region in total sales?

---

## 🔹 Dashboard Visualizations Used

### 1. Profit by Region & Customer Segment (Stacked Bar Chart)
Used to compare profitability across different regions while also understanding which customer segment contributes more or less profit.

### 2. Order Quantity & Sales Trend by Month (Bar-Line Combo Chart)
Helps analyze monthly purchasing patterns by comparing total order quantity with total sales trend over time.

### 3. Customer Profitability Analysis (Scatter Plot)
Used to identify customer-wise relationship between Sales and Profit and detect:
- High Sales, High Profit customers
- High Sales, Low Profit customers
- Low Sales, Low Profit customers

### 4. Sales Contribution by Region (Pie Chart)
Shows the percentage share of each region in overall company sales.

## 🔹 Key Insights Derived

- Some regions generate strong sales volume but comparatively lower profits.
- Certain customer segments contribute more consistently to profitability.
- Monthly order quantity and sales do not always move proportionally, indicating variation in average order value.
- A few customers generate high revenue but contribute limited profit margins.
- Sales contribution is concentrated within selected high-performing regions.
